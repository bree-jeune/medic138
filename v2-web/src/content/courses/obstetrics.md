Field Priority: Stabilize maternal circulation (IV access x2, fluid, blood products if ordered) and expedite transport to surgical capability.

Eclampsia/Pre-eclampsia

Eclampsia: Seizures (tonic-clonic) in a pregnant patient.

Pre-eclampsia: Hypertension (BP >140/90 or >160/110) + proteinuria or organ dysfunction (headache, visual changes, RUQ pain).

Goal: Seizure control. Magnesium Sulfate is the mainstay. Manage severe hypertension with Labetalol or Hydralazine.

Delivery and Cord Prolapse

Cord Prolapse: Cord presents before the baby's head. Leads to cord compression and rapid fetal hypoxia.

Management: Place mother in knee-chest position or Trendelenburg. Gloved hand must remain inside to push the presenting part off the cord. This cannot stop until C-section. - Preterm Labor: Contractions + cervical changes before 37 weeks. Transport to facility capable of NICU care.

Common Mistakes

Delaying Transport in Unstable Hemorrhage: Do not waste time attempting delivery or stabilization if the mother is crashing from bleeding. Get moving.

Ignoring Eclampsia Warning Signs: Treat persistent headache, visual changes, and severe hypertension aggressively to prevent seizure.

Failing to Treat Supine Hypotensive Syndrome: Positioning the mother flat on her back compresses the vena cava (at >20 weeks), dropping BP. Always use left lateral tilt or position the mother on her left side.

Checking Fetal Status Before Maternal Status: Maternal stability dictates fetal outcome. Treat Mom first.
