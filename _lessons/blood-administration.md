## Arrival Scenario

You're transporting a 42-year-old male from a rural ED to a trauma center. GSW to abdomen, emergent ex-lap, lost 2 liters in the OR. He's hypotensive (BP 82/50), tachy (HR 135), pale, and cold.

The surgeon hands you 4 units of PRBCs and 2 units of FFP. "He needs these on the way. Massive transfusion protocol. You good?"

**Because when you're moving blood, mistakes kill fast.**

---

## Blood Product Basics

### Packed Red Blood Cells (PRBCs)
- **What it does:** Carries oxygen, increases Hgb
- **Volume:** ~300 mL per unit
- **Raises Hgb by:** ~1 g/dL per unit
- **When to use:** Hemorrhage, severe anemia (Hgb < 7)

### Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)
- **What it does:** Replaces clotting factors
- **Volume:** ~250 mL per unit
- **When to use:** Massive transfusion, coagulopathy, warfarin reversal

### Platelets
- **What it does:** Stops bleeding, helps clotting
- **Volume:** ~300 mL per unit
- **When to use:** Thrombocytopenia (< 50K in bleeding patient)

---

## Massive Transfusion Protocol (MTP)

**1:1:1 Ratio:** 1 PRBCs : 1 FFP : 1 Platelets

**Why?** Giving only PRBCs dilutes clotting factors â†’ coagulopathy

**Activation criteria:**
- Loss of 1 blood volume in 24 hours
- > 4 units PRBCs in 1 hour
- Shock + ongoing bleeding

## Blood Compatibility

| Recipient | Can Receive |
|-----------|-------------|
| O- | O- only (Universal Donor) |
| O+ | O-, O+ |
| AB+ | All types (Universal Recipient) |

**In emergencies:** Use O- PRBCs, AB plasma

---

## Administration Rules

1. **Verify TWICE** (patient name, DOB, blood type, unit number)
2. **Blood tubing** with 170-micron filter (NEVER standard IV set)
3. **Use NS only** (No LR, No D5W)
4. **Blood warmer** for rapid transfusion
5. **Max hang time:** 4 hours per unit
6. **Rate:** Wide open in hemorrhage, 2-4 hours in stable patients

---

## Transfusion Reactions

### Acute Hemolytic (ABO incompatibility)
- **Signs:** Fever, back pain, hypotension, dark urine, shock
- **Action:** STOP immediately, NS, treat shock

### Febrile Non-Hemolytic
- **Signs:** Fever, chills
- **Action:** Slow/stop, acetaminophen

### Allergic
- **Signs:** Urticaria, itching, flushing
- **Action:** Stop, Benadryl

### Anaphylaxis
- **Signs:** Respiratory distress, angioedema, shock
- **Action:** Stop, epi 0.3-0.5 mg IM, airway management

### TRALI
- **Signs:** Acute respiratory distress, hypoxia, bilateral infiltrates
- **Action:** Stop, supportive care (O2, CPAP)

### TACO
- **Signs:** Dyspnea, HTN, JVD, crackles, pulmonary edema
- **Action:** Stop/slow, diuretics, CPAP
